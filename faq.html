AC Systems

What type of air conditioning system is best for my home?

The best system depends on several factors including your home's size, layout, insulation, climate, and budget. Central air systems are ideal for cooling entire homes, while ductless mini-splits offer flexibility for cooling specific zones. Window units and portable ACs are good for smaller spaces or temporary cooling. We recommend scheduling a free consultation with our experts who can assess your specific needs.

How long do air conditioning systems typically last?

With proper maintenance, central air conditioning systems typically last 15-20 years, while ductless mini-split systems can last up to 20 years. Window units and portable ACs generally last 8-10 years. Regular professional maintenance can significantly extend the lifespan of any system.

What size air conditioner do I need?

Air conditioner sizing is measured in BTUs (British Thermal Units) or tons. A properly sized unit is crucial for efficiency and comfort. As a general rule, you need about 20 BTUs per square foot of living space, but factors like ceiling height, insulation, window placement, and local climate also matter. Our technicians can perform a detailed load calculation to determine the exact size needed for your space.

What's the difference between a heat pump and a traditional AC system?

While traditional AC systems only provide cooling, heat pumps can both cool and heat your home by reversing the refrigeration cycle. In cooling mode, both systems work similarly, but heat pumps can also extract heat from outside air (even in cold temperatures) and move it inside during winter. Heat pumps are generally more energy-efficient for heating in moderate climates but may need supplemental heating in extremely cold regions.

Maintenance

How often should I service my air conditioner?

We recommend professional maintenance at least once a year, ideally in spring before the cooling season begins. For systems that provide both heating and cooling, bi-annual maintenance (spring and fall) is ideal. Regular maintenance ensures optimal performance, extends system lifespan, and can prevent costly breakdowns.

How often should I change my air filter?

Standard 1-inch filters should typically be changed every 1-3 months, depending on factors like pets, allergies, and air quality. Thicker premium filters (3-5 inches) may last 6-12 months. Check your filter monthly and replace it when it appears dirty. A clean filter improves air quality, system efficiency, and prevents unnecessary strain on your equipment.

What maintenance can I do myself, and what requires a professional?

Homeowners can handle basic maintenance like changing air filters, keeping outdoor units clear of debris, ensuring vents aren't blocked, and cleaning visible dust from registers. Professional maintenance should include refrigerant level checks, electrical component inspection, coil cleaning, system calibration, and other technical tasks that require specialized tools and expertise.

What are the benefits of a maintenance plan?

Our maintenance plans offer regular scheduled service, priority scheduling for repairs, discounts on parts and labor, extended warranty coverage, and peace of mind knowing your system is operating safely and efficiently. Maintenance plan customers typically save 20-30% on energy costs and experience fewer breakdowns compared to systems without regular maintenance.

Energy Efficiency

What SEER rating should I look for in a new air conditioner?

SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) measures cooling efficiency—higher numbers indicate greater efficiency. As of 2023, the minimum SEER rating for new installations is 14 in northern states and 15 in southern states. We typically recommend systems with SEER ratings of 16-18 for a good balance of efficiency and cost. High-efficiency models (20+ SEER) cost more upfront but can provide significant long-term energy savings, especially in hot climates with extended cooling seasons.

How can I reduce my cooling costs?

Several strategies can help reduce cooling costs: maintain your system regularly, use a programmable thermostat (set it 7-10°F higher when you're away), seal air leaks around windows and doors, add insulation, use ceiling fans, keep blinds closed during peak sun hours, avoid heat-generating activities during the hottest parts of the day, and consider upgrading to a more efficient system if yours is over 10 years old.

Are there tax credits or rebates available for energy-efficient systems?

Yes, various incentives are available for energy-efficient HVAC installations. Federal tax credits currently offer up to 30% of the cost for qualifying high-efficiency systems. Many utility companies also offer rebates ranging from $100-$1,000 depending on the system efficiency. State and local incentives may provide additional savings. Our team can help identify all available incentives during your consultation.

What's the most energy-efficient temperature to set my thermostat?

The U.S. Department of Energy recommends setting your thermostat to 78°F (26°C) when you're home and need cooling. Every degree below this setting increases energy consumption by approximately 3-5%. Using a programmable or smart thermostat to increase the temperature when you're away or sleeping can further reduce energy use without sacrificing comfort when you need it most.
